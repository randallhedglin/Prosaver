Part D: Saving and Loading Playlists


The final section of this Tutorial will help you with another useful feature of ProSaver Screensaver Manager, the ability to save your favorite screensaver playlists (based on theme, type, or anything you prefer) and then load them again later as needed (when your mood changes, the weather changes, or anything else makes you say, “Hey, I’d like some different screensavers today!”).  Here are some easy steps to follow:

 

1. With your favorite screensavers arranged in the Screensaver Playlist, click the Save to File button to bring up the Save Screensaver Playlist dialog box.  Enter a name (such as ‘Favorites’) in the ‘File name’ box and click Save.  It’s that easy!

 

2. Next, we’re going to do something daring, maybe even a little scary!  Click on the Clear List button, and click Yes in the warning box that opens.  Yes, your precious playlist is now gone!  (Don’t worry – it didn’t get far.  We’ll come back to that later.)

 

3. For now, click OK to close ProSaver, and also click OK in the box that pops up to warn you about the empty playlist.

 

4. Just for fun, as described in Part C of this Tutorial, preview the screensaver using the Windows Display Properties box.

 

5. You will now see the ProSaver Default Screensaver.  (Yeah, yeah.  So it’s a little boring.  But it does the job.)

 

6. When you’ve recovered from that excitement, open the ProSaver Main Window once again.

 

7. Now click on the Load from File button in the Current Playlist Box.

 

8. Find the playlist file you saved in step 1, click on it, and click Open.

 

9. There’s your playlist, safe and sound!  You see?  There was never anything to worry about.

 

 

Congratulations!  You have just completed Tutorial #2: Creating a Screensaver Playlist.  You are now qualified to make screensaver playlists with the experts.  You are also ready to move on to Tutorial #3: Setting Playback Options.
