Part C: Ratings, Descriptions, and Mini-Previews


Now that you know your way around the Screensaver Info Editor and the Themes Manager a little better, let’s see what other options are available for organizing your screensaver collection.  Here’s what to do:

 

1. Open the Screensaver Info Editor, as described in Part A of this Tutorial.

 

2. If you haven’t already done so, select a theme for this screensaver by clicking the arrow in the theme list and choosing one.

 

3. Now, do the same with the ‘Rating’ box:  If you like this screensaver, give it four stars.  If you really like it, give it five.  Or, if it’s not so great, give it a one.  The choice is all yours!

 

4. Next, click in the ‘Description’ box.  Type a little description for this screensaver.

 

5. Click OK and you are done!

 

6. Repeat this process for as many screensavers as you want to right now, and consider yourself organized.

 

7. Now, in the Screensaver List, use the header-bar buttons to re-arrange the screensavers by name, theme, or rating.  In this way, you can see your screensavers in any order that happens to be convenient at the time.

 

8. Here’s one more thing to try:  Back in the Screensaver Info Editor, click to check the ‘Turn off mini-preview’ box.

 

9. Click the Apply button and the Mini-Preview Window will go dark.

 

10. Click the box again to clear it, then click OK.  The preview will be back.

 

 

Congratulations!  You have just completed Tutorial #4: Organizing Your Screensavers.  Your screensaver collection will never be cluttered again.  (Unfortunately, we can’t say the same thing about your desk.)  You now have our permission to continue with Tutorial #5: Adding Music and Audio Playlists.
