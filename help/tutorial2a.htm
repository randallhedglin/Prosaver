Part A: Adding Screensavers


The most basic step involving in creating screensaver playlists is, of course, adding screensavers!  With ProSaver Screensaver Manager, this is very easy to do.  Just follow these steps:

 

1. Open the ProSaver Main Window.  The Screensavers Tab will be visible.

 

2. In the upper-left corner of the window, find the box labeled Available Screensavers, as shown above.

 

3. Here you will see the complete list of screensavers that you have available.

 

4. Using the scroll bar on the right-hand side of the Screensaver List, find one of your favorite screensavers and click on it.

 

5. You will notice that the Screensaver Info Box, just below the Screensaver List, comes to life, displaying information about the screensaver.  Also, the Mini-Preview Window on the bottom-right will show a preview of the screensaver.

 

6. Now, click the Add to Playlist button and watch in amazement as the screensaver jumps from the Screensaver List to the Screensaver Playlist.  (Didn’t we tell you this would be easy?)

 

7. Click the Clear Selection button and the screensaver will be de-selected, the Screensaver Info Box will go dark, and so will the Mini-Preview Window.

 

8. Repeat this process to add the rest of your favorites to the Screensaver Playlist.  (You don’t have to click the Clear Selection button each time.  We just thought you should know what it does.)

 

 

Now you have a basic understanding of how to use the Screensaver List.  Here are some other things you can try:

 

• Click the header-bar buttons at the top of the Screensaver List (they are labeled Name, Theme, and Rating) to re-arrange the Screensaver List.  The list will be sorted based on the button you click.

 

• Here’s a quick and easy way to add ratings to your screensavers:  Right-click on the name of the screensaver in the Screensaver List to display a popup menu.  Then, move the cursor to ‘Change Rating’ and select a rating from the submenu that appears.

 

• Now that you have some ratings, right-click the list again and un-check the ‘Show Rating Images’ option.  All of the rating stars will be replaced with text.  (This, of course, is very bland, so you may want to repeat the process to turn the star images back on.)

 

• Right-click the list again, and this time un-check the ‘Show Details’ option.  The Screensaver List will now display a simple list, with just the screensaver names and icons.  Let your mouse pointer hover over one of them and you will see a small popup with more information about the screensaver.  (Now you can set the list back to Details Mode, if you prefer.)

 

• You can also use the popup menu to add screensavers to the playlist and to open the Screensaver Info Editor.  (The Screensaver Info Editor is discussed in more detail in Tutorial #4: Organizing Your Screensavers.)
