Part A: The Change Music Dialog


Let’s start with something easy.  In Part A of this Tutorial, we will go through the process of adding a single music file to a screensaver.  Here’s what to do:

 

1. Open the ProSaver Main Window.

 

2. In the Available Screensavers Box, we are going to look for one of the default Windows screensavers.  You should find it under the name ‘Starfield’, ‘Flying Through Space’, or something similar.  When you find it, click on it.

 

3. Now, click the Preview button in the Current Selection Box.  Kinda boring, right?

 

4. Click on the Edit Info button to open the Screensaver Info Editor.

 

5. Click on the Change button, which will open the Change Music Dialog (shown above)

 

6. Here, you are given three options: ‘Load Single Audio File’, ‘Load ProSaver Audio File’, and ‘Set Audio to None’.  (You also have the convenient option to ‘Open Music Manager’ from here, in case you decide to create or edit a playlist at the last minute.)

 

7. As stated above, for now we are just going to add a single audio file, so click the Load Single Audio File button.

 

8. If you haven’t loaded any audio files yet, the ProSaver sample audio folder should now be displayed.  (If not, you can usually find it at ‘C:\Program Files\ProSaver\Audio’.)

 

9. In this folder, find the file ‘Space Battle.mid’.  Click on it, then click Open.

 

10. Click OK to close the Screensaver Info Editor.

 

11. You will now see the name ‘Space Battle.mid’ in the Screensaver Info Box.  Click the Preview button, and this time it will be much more exciting!

 

 

And that’s how to use the Change Music Dialog.  Now we can move on to the next section.
