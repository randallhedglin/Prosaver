Part B: Themes and the Themes Manager


ProSaver Screensaver Manager comes pre-loaded with a number of useful themes, or categories, which you can use when organizing your favorite screensavers.  You began working with these in Part A of this Tutorial.  However, there may be some screensavers that do not fit into any of these categories, or you may prefer to use some different ones.  Situations like this are when you need to use the ProSaver Themes Manager.  Here’s how:

 

1. Open the ProSaver Main Window.

 

2. Click on the Playback Options Tab, near the top of the window.

 

3. In the bottom-left corner of the General Settings section, you will find the Open Themes Manager button.  Click it to open the Themes Manager.

 

4. Scroll up and down through the list to see what is available.

 

5. Now, let’s add a theme to the list.  Click the New button.

 

6. Type the word ‘Cows’ into the box and click OK.

 

7. You will now see ‘Cows’ in the list, in alphabetical order.

 

8. Now, scroll down and find the ‘Nature’ theme.  Click on it, then click the Delete button.

 

9. A window will pop up, asking if you really want to delete the selected theme.  Click Yes.

 

10. The theme ‘Nature’ has been removed from the list.

 

11. Next, scroll up and click on the ‘Animals’ theme.

 

12. Click the Rename button.

 

13. In the text box, change ‘Animals’ to ‘Critters’ and click OK.  The theme in the list has been renamed!

 

14. As much fun as all of this has been, let’s just set the list back to the way it was.  Click the Reset Defaults button.

 

15. Click Yes in the window that comes up, and the list will be back to normal.

 

16. Click OK to close the Themes Manager.

 

 

Well, that wasn’t so bad, was it?  You can also conveniently access the Themes Manager directly from the Screensaver Info Editor by clicking the Edit Themes button.  When you are done experimenting, we will move on to the next section.
