Part D: The Music Manager


The ProSaver Music Manager is the place to go for all of your music needs.  (Well, at least for all of your music needs that involve screensavers.)  Here, you can do the following:

 

• Look for music files (in MP3, WAV, MIDI, and many other formats) on your computer’s hard drive(s).

 

• Create, edit, save, and load playlists made up of these music files, which can then be selected as background music for any of your screensavers.

 

• Preview your songs before adding them to your playlists.

 

• Set options for music playback, including Playback Controls and Synthesizer Options.

 

 

The Music Manager is discussed in much more detail in Tutorial #5: Adding Music and Audio Playlists.

 

For now, give this a try:

 

1. Open the ProSaver Main Window.

 

2. Open the Playback Options Tab, located near the top of the window.

 

3. Click the Open Music Manager button.  You should now see the Music Manager.

 

4. In most cases, the ProSaver sample audio folder will already be displayed.  If not, use the Audio File List to locate the folder (usually ‘C:\Program Files\ProSaver\Audio’).

 

5. Select an audio file of your choice and click on it.

 

6. Some information about the file will be displayed in the Current Selection Box.

 

7. Click the Play button to listen to the file.

 

8. Listen to a few more sample files.

 

9. Close the window when you are done.

 

 

Congratulations!  You have just completed Tutorial #1: Introduction to ProSaver.  You are now familiar with the basic features of ProSaver Screensaver Manager.  Next, you can move on to Tutorial #2: Creating a Screensaver Playlist.
